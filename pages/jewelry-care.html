Jewelry Care Guide

Expert tips to keep your jewelry looking beautiful for generations

Fine jewelry is an investment that can last for generations when properly cared for. Our comprehensive care guide will help you maintain the beauty and integrity of your precious pieces.

Cleaning Sterling Silver

Why Silver Tarnishes

Sterling silver naturally tarnishes when exposed to air and certain chemicals. This is a normal process that occurs when silver reacts with sulfur-containing substances in the air.

Cleaning Method

  1. Line a bowl with aluminum foil, shiny side up
  2. Add 1 tablespoon of baking soda and 1 tablespoon of salt
  3. Pour in hot water (not boiling) to dissolve the mixture
  4. Place your silver jewelry on the foil, ensuring it touches the foil
  5. Let sit for 5-10 minutes
  6. Remove, rinse thoroughly with water, and dry with a soft cloth

Pro Tip

For heavily tarnished pieces, repeat the process or use a silver polishing cloth for stubborn areas.

Gold Jewelry Maintenance

Caring for Different Gold Types

Gold jewelry requires different care depending on its karat and color. Higher karat gold (18K, 24K) is softer and more susceptible to scratches, while lower karat gold (10K, 14K) is more durable but may tarnish slightly over time.

Cleaning Method

  1. Mix a few drops of mild dish soap with warm water
  2. Soak your gold jewelry for 15-30 minutes
  3. Gently scrub with a soft toothbrush, focusing on crevices
  4. Rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water
  5. Pat dry with a soft, lint-free cloth

Pro Tip

Remove gold jewelry before swimming in chlorinated pools or using household cleaners, as these can damage the metal over time.

Gemstone Care

Understanding Gemstone Hardness

Different gemstones have varying levels of hardness and require specific care. While diamonds are extremely durable (10 on the Mohs scale), pearls and opals are much softer (2.5-6) and need gentle handling.

General Cleaning Guidelines

  • Diamonds: Soak in warm, soapy water and scrub gently with a soft brush
  • Sapphires & Rubies: Similar to diamonds, but avoid ultrasonic cleaners if stones have been treated
  • Emeralds: Wipe gently with a damp cloth; avoid soaking or ultrasonic cleaners
  • Pearls: Wipe with a soft, damp cloth after wearing; never soak or use cleaners
  • Opals: Clean with a soft cloth and mild soap; avoid heat and prolonged water exposure

Pro Tip

Store gemstone jewelry separately to prevent scratching. Softer stones should be wrapped in soft cloth or placed in individual pouches.

Proper Jewelry Storage

How you store your jewelry is just as important as how you clean it. Proper storage prevents tangling, scratching, and premature tarnishing.

Temperature & Humidity

Store jewelry in a cool, dry place with consistent temperature. Avoid bathrooms where humidity fluctuates dramatically.

Separate Storage

Store pieces separately to prevent scratching. Use individual pouches, compartments, or anti-tarnish strips for silver.

Necklace Storage

Hang necklaces to prevent tangling, or lay flat with chains fully extended in a jewelry tray.

Ring Storage

Use ring slots or individual compartments to prevent stones from scratching other pieces.

Pro Tip

Place a small silica gel packet in your jewelry box to absorb excess moisture and prevent tarnishing.
